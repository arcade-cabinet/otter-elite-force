# OTTER: ELITE FORCE — Lore Bible

## The Copper-Silt Reach

A vast river-jungle delta where two civilizations collide. Warm water channels thread through mangrove forests, connecting fishing villages, salvage yards, and ancient mud-brick outposts. Before the occupation, otters and reptiles traded freely along the waterways. Now the channels are fortified, the villages are garrisoned, and the Reach is a war zone.

---

## OTTER ELITE FORCE (OEF)

**Motto:** "Through Mud and Water"

The Otter Elite Force is a guerrilla liberation army fighting to reclaim the Copper-Silt Reach from Scale-Guard occupation. Small, fast, and resourceful — they lack the Scale-Guard's numbers and armor but make up for it with engineering ingenuity, squad tactics, and sheer determination.

### Command Structure

- **FOXHOUND** — Radio callsign for OEF field intelligence. Not a person — it's the encrypted channel that relays orders from command to field operators. The voice in your ear. Calm, precise, encyclopedic knowledge of Scale-Guard deployments.

- **Sgt. Bubbles** — Field commander. Rambo-otter. Red bandana, battle scars, teal eye glint. Led the first wave into the Reach. Inspires loyalty through action, not rank. Carries a mangrove-wood fighting stick.

- **Gen. Whiskers** — OEF supreme commander. Grizzled veteran of the First Reach War. Dark beret, cigar, three campaign medals. Captured by Scale-Guard early in the campaign (Mission 4 rescue). Takes over briefings after rescue.

- **Cpl. Splash** — Aquatic recon specialist. Young, eager, goggles-on-forehead. Grew up diving the deep channels. Rescued in Mission 8. Unlocks Diver unit.

- **Sgt. Fang** — Heavy weapons and siege specialist. Built like a dam. Helmet, jaw scars, thousand-yard stare. Rescued in Mission 12. Bonus damage vs buildings.

- **Medic Marina** — Field medic. White cap with red cross. Gentle eyes that have seen too much. Rescued in Mission 10. Heals nearby units.

- **Pvt. Muskrat** — Demolitions expert. Cap with bomb insignia. Nervous energy, steady hands. Mission 14 hero mission — plants charges behind enemy lines.

## SCALE-GUARD MILITIA

**Motto:** "The Reach is Ours"

The Scale-Guard is an occupying reptilian military force that seized the Copper-Silt Reach after the otters' alliance fragmented. They are larger, stronger, better-armored — but slow, rigid, and overconfident. Their doctrine is area denial: siphons drain the water, walls block the channels, patrols lock down the crossings.

### Command Structure

- **Kommandant Ironjaw** — Supreme commander of the Scale-Guard occupation. A massive saltwater crocodile with a prosthetic iron jaw (lost the original to a mortar in the First Reach War). Cold, methodical, patient. Believes the otters will exhaust themselves against his fortifications. Appears in final campaign missions as the strategic mind behind the occupation. The Serpent King title is propaganda — he's no king, just a very effective general.

- **Captain Scalebreak** — Field commander of the Causeway garrison. An albino alligator — pale scales, pink eyes, terrifyingly calm. Runs the ambush network along the main supply route. The voice you hear on intercepted Scale-Guard radio. Player encounters him in Mission 2 (Causeway escort) and Mission 6 (wave defense).

- **Warden Fangrot** — Prison camp commandant. A bloated, scarred caiman who runs the compound where Gen. Whiskers is held. Sadistic but lazy — his guards are poorly trained but numerous. Mission 4 antagonist.

- **Venom** — Scale-Guard sniper-assassin. A king cobra (not a crocodilian — the Scale-Guard recruits across species). Silent, patient, deadly at range. Appears as a special unit in stealth missions. The only Scale-Guard soldier the otters genuinely fear.

- **The Broodmother** — Scale-Guard logistics commander. A massive monitor lizard who runs the spawning pools and supply lines. Not a fighter — an administrator who keeps the occupation fed and reinforced. Destroying her supply depots is a recurring campaign objective.

### Scale-Guard Doctrine

The Scale-Guard fights through **occupation and attrition**, not maneuver:

1. **Fortify** — Walls, siphons, and watchtowers create killing fields
2. **Deny** — Siphons drain waterways, cutting otter supply routes
3. **Patrol** — Scout Lizards maintain surveillance; Gators run ambush routes
4. **Overwhelm** — When threatened, spawn waves from Predator Nests
5. **Endure** — Croc Champions absorb punishment and keep fighting

The Scale-Guard's weakness is inflexibility. They follow doctrine. They don't adapt. A creative commander (you) can exploit gaps in their patrol routes, bait their ambushes, and destroy their infrastructure faster than they can rebuild.

## THE CONFLICT

The Copper-Silt Reach was neutral territory for generations — shared fishing grounds, trading posts, seasonal settlements. The Scale-Guard militarized it after the Otter Alliance fractured in a political crisis (which Kommandant Ironjaw may have engineered). Now the otters want it back.

This isn't a war about ideology or magic. It's about territory, resources, and the river crossings that connect everything. The otters fight for their homes. The Scale-Guard fights to hold what they took.

### Campaign Arc

**Chapter 1: First Landing** — OEF establishes a beachhead. Learns the basics. Discovers the Scale-Guard is better entrenched than intelligence suggested.

**Chapter 2: Deep Operations** — Deeper into occupied territory. Missions get harder. Weather, water traversal, and defense missions test the player.

**Chapter 3: Turning Tide** — Rescue operations for captured heroes. Each rescue unlocks new capabilities. The tide begins to shift.

**Chapter 4: Final Offensive** — Direct assault on Scale-Guard headquarters. Multi-base operations, boss encounters, the final reckoning against Kommandant Ironjaw.
